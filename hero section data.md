## 3. Objective

The primary objective of ICETCS 2026 is to provide a global platform for researchers, academicians, industry professionals, and policymakers to converge, share insights, and collaboratively explore the latest advancements and emerging trends in the field of cybersecurity. The conference aims to facilitate discussion on cutting-edge research, innovative technologies, and practical solutions addressing evolving cyber threats in an interconnected world. By fostering interdisciplinary dialogue and knowledge exchange, the conference seeks to contribute to robust cybersecurity strategies, enhance academia–industry collaboration, and strengthen collective global resilience against cyber threats.

**Note:** All accepted papers will be published in Lecture Notes in Electrical Engineering — the Scopus-indexed Springer proceedings.

### B. Preparation of Manuscript

**Summary:** Manuscripts for Springer proceedings should adhere to the provided template, ensuring consistent formatting (font size, margin settings, section styles). Authors must include a concise title, author details, abstract, keywords, and clearly organized content (introduction, methodology, results, conclusion). Figures, tables, and references should follow Springer's guidelines for high-quality visuals and proper citation styles.

In exceptional circumstances, authors must contact the **Publication Chair, Prof. Dr. Arafatur Rahman** (email redirected via [contact form](https://ritechs.org/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection)) — an extra charge will apply for each additional page.

#### Structure of the Paper

1. **Title** — Should clearly indicate the problem, proposed solution/approach, and target domain.
2. **Abstract** — Should briefly include background, problem statement, aim/objective, methodology or contribution, and expected research or social impact.
3. **Background / Introduction** — Introduce the topic, explain the problem with relevant evidence/statistics, state aim and objectives, highlight main contributions, and outline the paper's organisation.
4. **Related Work** — Discuss recent and relevant studies, organize thematically where possible, and identify how the proposed work differs from existing research.
5. **Methodology** — Describe the complete research process, framework, model, system design, or experimental procedure. A block diagram, architecture, workflow, or phase-wise explanation is encouraged.
6. **Results and Discussion** — Present experimental setup, findings, analysis, and interpretation of results; explain significance of findings.
7. **Conclusion** — Summarise the work, key findings, contributions, limitations, and possible future directions.
8. **References** — At least **15 recent references**, preferably from IEEE Xplore, Elsevier, Springer, ACM, or other reputable academic publishers. References from the last five years are encouraged.
9. **Paper Format** — Must follow the **Springer conference templates**; approximately **15 pages**.
10. **Similarity / Plagiarism** — Similarity index must not exceed **10%** (excluding references). Block similarity will not be accepted.

**Double-Blind Paper:** Authors must NOT include names, affiliations, postal addresses, or email addresses in the initial manuscript.

**Manuscript Template:** Must follow standard Springer conference templates (max 15 pages). Template available at: [Manuscript Guideline](https://www.springer.com/gp/authors-editors/conference-proceedings/conference-proceedings-guidelines)

### C. Plagiarism & Multiple Submission Policy

- **Plagiarism:** All papers must present original, unpublished work. Suspected plagiarism cases will be handled per ICETCS 2026 and Springer policies on plagiarism, copyright infringement, moral rights violations, and simultaneous submissions. Springer's plagiarism policy is referenced on the Springer site.
- **Multiple Submissions:** ICETCS 2026 and Springer strictly prohibit manuscripts under simultaneous review for any other conference, journal, or publication outlet. Confirmed cases will result in prompt rejection and appropriate disciplinary action. Springer's multiple-submissions policy is referenced on the Springer site.

### D. Copyright

All accepted papers will be published in the Scopus-indexed Springer proceedings, with Springer holding the copyright. A separate Springer copyright form must be completed for each individual paper.
